I like to listen to a lot of punk, my favorite punk genre is crust punk. Crust punk is a mixture of anarcho punk and extreme metal that originated in englad in the early to mid 1980s. The songs from the genre often discuss anrachism, environmentalism, feminism, animal rights, and anti-capitialism. The instrumentation in crust punk is a mix of punk and metal riffs for guitar. Drumming is usually done at a fast pace utualizing d-beats. And for bass..... who cares about the bass. Notable bands from the genre include Doom, Dystopia, Disrupt, Nausea, Extreme Noise Terror, and Antisect.

Going back to the topic of music, another genre that I like to listen to is grindcore. Similar to crust punk, grindcore is a mixture of punk and extreme metal, except it takes more inspiration from more metal genres such as thrash metal, and death metal. Because of that, it is sometimes considered a metal genre. But its punk roots sometimes overshadows the metal roots, so some people may argue that its a punk genre. But were here to talk the genre, not debate on whether its a metal or punk genre. Grindcore, like crust punk, originated in the uk in the 1980s. The band Napalm Death are credited for laying the ground work for the genre with the release of theier debut album Scum in 1987. Instrumentation is similar to crust punk, except everything is played faster. Drumming is done at a faster pace utilizing a drum beat called blast beats. A characteristic on some songsin the genre is the microsong, where a song is only a few seconds long. Due to how short the songs are, grindcore albums are usually short, typically being only 15-20 minutes. Lyrical material is similar to crust punk, except if you go into its 2 microgenres which I wont go into detail due to its non school friendly lyrical themes. Notable band in the genre include Agathocles, Nasum, Insect Warfare, Pig Destroyer, and of course Napalm Death.
